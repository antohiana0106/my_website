Zoetrope

Zoetrope, powered by an Arduino, that shows a flying dragon as it spins and looks up into the into the upper part. When the aluminum foil is touched, the zoetrope starts to rotate. The direction of rotation can be changed with the switch, while the duration of spinning can be changed with the knob on the right. The sensor is a touch sensor and the actuator is a motor.

Cat robot

A carboard cat that looks as a laser pointer on the screen. The sensors used for this project are two light sensors and a button and the actuators are a servo and an LED. When the button is pressed the LED lights up, the background of the Processing screen changes, and some notes on a virtual synth are being played. The laser pointer on screen and the servo in the cat's neck is controlled by the two light sensors that follow the laser movement. Serial and MIDI data is used to communicate between Arduino, Processing, and Dexed (the virtual synth program).

Wallart CO2 meter

Turning a CO2 detector into an aesthetic design that actually makes people aware of the gradual increase of CO2 indoors. Using a color signal, the 3D art changes color from white to red depending on the CO2 level, from normal (white) to dangerous (red). It measures not only CO2 in ppm, but also temperature and humidity (which are also measured with a normal CO2 detector). The statistics can be checked on a web server by scanning a QR code. The hardware used is an ESP8266 chip, CO2 meter as the sensor, and LEDs as the actuator, all covered by a 3D printed design.

XPEDAL

XPedal is a game-changing device designed to keep esports players active and injury-free – a desk pedal built to keep gamers moving while they game. It reduces RSI risks, improves blood circulation—without distracting you from your matches. It’s packed with smart tech that syncs with your PC and adapts in real time. When the action heats up, the pedal eases off; during breaks, it adds resistance to keep you moving. Stop pedaling, and it gives you a gentle sound reminder to resume.
